FIXED DEPOSIT:

A fixed deposit (FD) is a financial instrument provided by Bank/ NBFC/ Corporates which provides investors with a higher rate of interest than a regular savings account, It comes with a Predefined maturity date or different interest payment options like Monthly. Quarterly, Half yearly, Annually or Cumulative. We at GADE Capital Provide fixed deposits from NBFC/ Corporates which are rated AAA or AA+ or above by CRISIL or ICRA.

Advantages Of Fixed deposit

Fixed Deposit enable Investor to earn a higher rate of interest on their surplus funds .
Fixed Deposit Is an onetime investment which can be locked for a longer duration to generate better returns.
Fixed Deposit Investor is given a fixed deposit receipt for his investment.
Fixed Deposit can be enchased or renewed for a further period on maturity as per requirement of the investor.
Fixed Deposit interest can be credited to the Savings account specified by the depositor on a monthly basis or on a quarterly basis.
Fixed deposit with cumulative option reinvestment the interest is compounded to the principal amount on a quarterly/half yearly basis.
Tax is deducted at source, from the interest on Fixed Deposits as per the Income Tax Act, 1961.

Loan Facility:

Interest rate charged on the loan given on a fixed deposit is usually 2-2.5% above the interest on fixed deposit.
In order to access funds at a short interval, one can avail a loan against Fixed deposit held. It is given in the form of an overdraft against your deposited amount.
This is an alternative given to customer instead of breaking the deposit prematurely. Loan against fixed deposit is a great option for those looking to avail a loan at a better rate when compared to personal loans
Investor will continue to earn interest on the deposit though you have availed a loan against it.

Eligibility:

All Resident individuals (Including Minors) and HUF are eligible to invest in Fixed Deposit.
Some Banks & NBFCs accept Fixed deposit from corporates & trusts
NRI's are allowed to invest in Fixed deposit through NRO Account , Subject to acceptance by Bank/NBFC
